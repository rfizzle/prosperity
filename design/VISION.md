# Prosperity — Vision

> *"Every chest, yours to discover."* — the loot overhaul that makes the world's
> treasure personal, and rewards you for going out to find it.

## The promise

Prosperity takes over the loot in Minecraft's naturally-generated containers —
the chests, barrels, shulkers, dispensers, and minecarts that spawn in dungeons,
temples, mineshafts, villages, and structures. With it installed, every one of
those containers holds a fresh, private haul for *you*. The friend who raided the
dungeon an hour ago left it full for you; the server veteran can't strip every
stronghold before you arrive. And the further you travel from the center of the
world, the richer those hauls become — so exploration always pays. Your own base
chests and storage are never touched: this is about the treasure the world
places, not the crates you build.

## The arc of a world

In your first hours the change is quiet but constant. Every ruined-portal chest,
every village smithy, every shipwreck is yours alone, and a soft gold sparkle
marks the ones you haven't opened yet. You clear your local area knowing nothing
was skimmed before you got there.

By the mid game you feel the pull outward. A thousand blocks from the center the
loot noticeably improves, and it keeps climbing the further you push. You start
planning expeditions — a compass that points to the nearest treasure you haven't
claimed, a badge in the corner naming the tier of loot you're standing in. The
map stops being a place you've "done" and becomes a gradient of reward.

Late game is the deep frontier: ten thousand blocks out, where stacks come in the
largest sizes and the rarest drops surface. Clearing every container in a
structure earns a bonus. And if a server turns on restocking, the strongholds you
emptied months ago are worth the return trip.

## The features, as you'll play them

### Loot that's yours alone
- **What it is** — Every naturally-generated loot container rolls its own private
  contents for each player. Chests, trapped chests, barrels, end-city shulkers,
  temple dispensers, and the chest and hopper minecarts in mineshafts all count.
- **How you use it** — Just open them, the way you always have. You see your own
  loot, with the full lid animation and sound; another player opening the same
  chest sees theirs.
- **What changes for you** — No more empty dungeon because someone beat you to it.
  The first visitor no longer decides what everyone else gets — the race to loot
  first is over.

### Loot that grows with distance
- **What it is** — The world is divided into five distance bands measured from its
  center: Local, Frontier, Wilderness, Outlands, and Depths. The further out, the
  larger the stacks and the better the odds of rare items.
- **How you use it** — Travel. Local loot (within 1,000 blocks) is vanilla-sized;
  cross into Frontier at 1,000 blocks for 1.5× stacks, Wilderness at 3,000 for 2×,
  Outlands at 6,000 for 2.75×, and Depths past 10,000 for 3.5× and the best odds.
  The same bands scale the drops from hostile mobs you kill, your fishing catches,
  and trial-chamber rewards.
- **What changes for you** — Distance becomes a reward curve instead of a chore. A
  zombie in the deep frontier drops more than one at spawn; a far-flung chest is
  worth the journey.

### A sparkle on what you haven't claimed
- **What it is** — Every container you haven't looted yet wears a gentle gold
  sparkle you can spot from across a room.
- **How you use it** — Look around. The sparkle shows through walls when you're
  within 8 blocks and out to 48 blocks in the open, fading at the edge of range.
  Loot the container and its sparkle winks out.
- **What changes for you** — You never wonder whether you've already cleared a
  chest, and hidden treasure announces itself instead of blending into the build.

### The Prospector's Compass
- **What it is** — A gold-cased compass whose needle points to the nearest
  container *you* personally haven't looted.
- **How you use it** — Find one in the loot of Frontier-and-beyond chests (about a
  1-in-100 find) — there's no recipe, it's a treasure in its own right. Hold it and
  follow the needle; loot the target and it swings to the next. With nothing
  unlooted nearby it spins freely, like a compass in the Nether.
- **What changes for you** — Treasure-hunting gains a direction. You can sweep a
  structure or a stretch of wilderness without backtracking or guessing.

### Your loot tier at a glance
- **What it is** — A small badge in the corner of the screen naming the distance
  tier you're standing in, colored by rank.
- **How you use it** — It's just there while you play. Hold the Peek Loot Detail
  key (Left Alt by default, rebindable under Controls) to expand it into a full
  panel: your exact distance, your progress toward the next tier, the whole tier
  ladder, and the unlooted containers around you.
- **What changes for you** — You always know how good the loot is where you stand,
  and how far to the next step up — without opening a menu or breaking stride.

## Staying in control

Every feature can be switched off, and the defaults assume a solo or small-group
player who changes nothing. The tier badge, the sparkle indicators, distance
scaling, mob and fishing and trial-chamber scaling, loot notifications, and the
structure-completion bonus can each be turned off on their own. `/prosperity info`
tells any player their current tier and multipliers; `/prosperity stats` reports
their lifetime loot totals.

Several extras stay **off** until a server owner opts in: **loot refresh**, which
restocks a player's containers after a cooldown (7 in-game days by default) so old
structures are worth revisiting; **party loot**, which lets teammates share one
haul per container; **container protection**, which slows anyone trying to break a
chest others still have loot in; and quiet housekeeping that reclaims the stored
loot of players long absent from a server. Server owners can also exclude specific
containers so they stay plain vanilla.

## Better together

Prosperity stands completely on its own — with no other mods installed, nothing is
missing. When its Concord siblings are present, the loot gets richer:

- **Tribulation** — where the world is more dangerous, chests and slain mobs pay
  out more, and tough enemies far from spawn drop gear to match.
- **Meridian** — enchanted books from the deep frontier roll live, distance-scaled
  enchantments, the strongest reserved for the deepest tier.
- **A party or teams mod** (Open Parties and Claims, FTB Teams) — party loot can
  follow your real party instead of a scoreboard team.
- **Jade or WTHIT** — look at a container to see its loot status, its tier, and any
  refresh timer.
- **EMI, REI, or JEI** — browse a searchable index of what each structure's loot
  can drop, tier by tier.

None of these are ever required.

## What Prosperity will never do

- **Touch your own storage.** The chests and barrels you place stay shared and
  vanilla; only the world's naturally-generated loot becomes private.
- **Change how the world looks or generates.** No new blocks, no retextured
  containers, no altered structures — a Prosperity chest is a vanilla chest.
- **Reach beyond loot.** It won't overhaul difficulty (that's Tribulation),
  enchanting (that's Meridian), trading, or progression, and it adds no new
  dimensions.
- **Require anything but Fabric.** No companion mod is ever mandatory.
- **Clutter your screen.** One small badge you can turn off, one sparkle you can
  turn off, and nothing that captures your mouse or interrupts your game.
